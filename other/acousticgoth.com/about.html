Beck Siàn

Beck first came to the UK from Australia in 2006 and spent three years touring both solo and with such names as Keith Potger (The Seekers), Julie Felix, Sheila Chandra, The Strawbs and Kathryn Roberts & Sean Lakeman. She has performed in venues as diverse as castles, abbeys, pubs, theatres, festivals and folk clubs. Beck then took some time away from the live gigs to record two albums in Shropshire and mid Wales and has remained in the UK, in order to continue her live work.

 

Beck's music has been described as "Ethereal Ghost-Folk", with a tendency towards the darker, supernatural ballads and nostalgic songs of love and loss, whether performing her original compositions or stirring interpretations of traditional and Celtic songs.

Her voice, which has been described by many top musicians as simply astonishing, bears some resemblance to her famed cousin, Kate Bush (and Beck often credits Kate as being a terrific influence on her through her career). However Beck has found her own voice, moving divinely between heartbreakingly wistful and an incredible soprano, she creates the perfect atmosphere to suit the ghostly, dark tales of her songs.

Perhaps surprisingly (although not once you know she's a Celtic-Aussie and is part Welsh and part Irish), she has a wicked and vibrant sense of humour often developing a direct and intimate rapport with her listeners.

 
  Jonathan Kershaw

Jonathan is a guitarist/songwriter originally from Yorkshire in northern England but now based on the south coast. Although known in various areas for his rock playing, he has quietly been applying his talents to an altogether different sound.

His debut album "Bizarre Tales"Â (released in 2012) is a serene blend of strong, traditional Celtic and folk sounds together with classical and rock influences.Although some of his material could be related to the folkier moments of rock giants such as

 
 
 

 Led Zeppelin, Rush or Jethro Tull, this does not fully quantify the complete spectrum of his sound.Intertwining guitar parts maintain a curiously ethereal feeling of mysticism with pieces that move through a variety of emotions.

Of his live show, Jonathan had this to say, "The live shows are often an intimate affair - especially if I'm playing solo or duet. I like to try and keep the crowd involved. The stage can be a lonely place when you're up by yourself and as much of the "Bizarre Tales" album is instrumental, I think it's important to still try and engage listeners vocally. So we try and have fun with call-backs and some light-hearted banter. Normally we end up with laughs and good vibes...normally! I also like to try and use lights and visuals to add to the experience..."
