Celtic Folk - Traditional - Acoustic

"...Evoking thoughts of the Celtic wilderness, times and loves through the power of the acoustic guitar..."

“Remnants” is the latest release by Jonathan Kershaw and delivers the traditional, Celtic side of his repertoire. It is in no way intended to be a follow up to his debut album “Bizarre Tales” but was simply inspired by requests for recordings of these pieces used during live performances; they are collated here on one album.

All the pieces are arranged for solo acoustic guitar (although several pieces actually use twin guitars to enhance the sound). The pieces provide an enticing and entwining web of fleet melody lines and bouncing bass line movements; whether it be in the jovial jigs of “The Blarney Pilgrim” and “The Killmoulis jig”, the melancholy “Pretty Girl Milking A Cow” or the haunting “Inisheer Air…”

The special edition of "Remnants" (only available as a CD) also contains a bonus track featuring the outstanding vocal ability of Beck Sian. Beck is cousin to English Singer Kate Bush and provides an incredibly haunting rendition of one of Jonathan's own tunes, "Clocks & Mirrors"; the original of which can be found on his album "Bizarre Tales".
Album Details Biography
Artist: Jonathan Kershaw Please follow this link to the Biography page of this site for a fuller description.

Jonathan Kershaw is a guitarist/songwriter originally from Yorkshire in northern England but now based on the south coast. Although known in various areas for his rock playing and indeed having played with members of bands including Red Hot Chili Peppers, Skunk Anansie and Cradle of Filth, he has quietly been applying his talents to an altogether different sound.

His music, epitomised in his debut album “Bizarre Tales” (released in 2012) is a serene blend of strong, traditional Celtic and folk sounds together with classical and rock influences. Although some of his material could be related to the folkier moments of rock giants such as Led Zeppelin, Rush or Jethro Tull, this does not fully quantify the complete spectrum of his sound. Intertwining guitar parts maintain a curiously ethereal feeling of mysticism with pieces that move through a variety of emotions.
